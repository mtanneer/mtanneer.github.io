⚽ Read this first: the 2026 World Cup overlaps your trip

The FIFA World Cup runs across North America this summer, and both the San Francisco Bay Area and Seattle are host cities — right inside your travel window. With Seattle now your final stop, the timing matters more than before:

  • Seattle (Jun 29 – Jul 2): A Round-of-32 knockout match is at Lumen Field on Wednesday, July 1 (afternoon kickoff). Downtown will be packed and hotels pricey around those nights — book your Seattle hotel as early as possible.
  • San Francisco (Jun 24–27): There's a Bay Area match the evening of Thursday June 25 at Levi's Stadium (Santa Clara, ~40 mi south). Expect region-wide hotel demand and Uber surges — book the SF hotel now too.
  • Silver lining: Free fan festivals and big-screen watch parties light up both downtowns — a fun, low-cost evening if you want to soak up the atmosphere on a match day.

Your Dietary Key

Every food pick below is vegetarian. The one nuance I've planned around: Mom & Sister avoid eggs in savory food (desserts with egg are fine), while you eat eggs freely.

Spots marked egg-free easy have plenty of fully egg-free savory mains — think South Indian, Sri Lankan, Ethiopian, Thai, Chinese, and Gujarati kitchens, where egg-free is the default rather than a special request. At any other listed spot, egg-free mains exist but are worth a quick confirm with the server. I've leaned the dinner picks toward the easy ones so nobody's ever stuck reading fine print.

Leg Two · The City of Roses & Its Wild Backyard

Portland Base

Wed Jun 11 – Wed Jun 24 · Home base · The heart of the trip — city days, gentle day trips by car, and built-in rest. June is peak rose season and prime waterfall flow.
FRI · JUN 12 Roses, Gardens & the World's Best Bookstore City · Gentle
Morning — Washington Park

An easy first full day. You've timed this perfectly: mid-June is peak bloom at the International Rose Test Garden (free, gentle terraced paths, sweeping city views). Right above it, the Portland Japanese Garden is one of the finest outside Japan — serene and beautifully maintained.

Afternoon

Drive up to Pittock Mansion for the panoramic view to Mt. Hood, then downtown to Powell's City of Books — a full city block of books, color-coded by room.

Evening

Dinner at Mirisata egg-free easy, a worker-owned Sri Lankan curry house (rice & curry plates, jackfruit, naturally egg-free), or Lalibela egg-free easy for communal Ethiopian platters on injera.

SAT · JUN 13 Saturday Market & Slow Neighborhoods City · Gentle
Morning

The Portland Saturday Market on the waterfront — crafts, local food, easy browsing — and the year-round PSU Farmers Market.

Afternoon

Wander the Alberta Arts District and Mississippi Avenue — indie shops, murals, and Portland's famous food-cart pods (everyone picks their own). Dip into the coffee religion; for pure whimsy, the Rimsky-Korsakoffee House in a creaky Victorian where the tables quietly move.

SUN · JUN 14 Flex / Rest Day Open · Your Call
The idea

A genuine breather after travel and two city days, before the day-trip stretch. Sleep in, revisit a favorite, or pick something low-key: the McMenamins Kennedy School (an old schoolhouse turned brewpub-cinema you can wander), or a stroll on the Eastbank Esplanade river path.

MON · JUN 15 Columbia River Gorge — Waterfall Alley Day Trip · Easy Walks
The Plan

Thirty minutes east into one of America's great waterfall corridors. Drive the Historic Columbia River Highway, stopping at Vista House at Crown Point, then a string of falls with short, mostly flat paths: Latourell Falls, Bridal Veil Falls, and the icon — Multnomah Falls, with the photogenic Benson Bridge a short stroll up. Add neighboring Wahkeena Falls if you have energy. Lunch in Hood River, then loop home.

⚠️ 2026 Gorge logistics — don't skip

  • Timed-entry permit (~$2) required to drive the Waterfall Corridor late May–early Sept. Book online in advance — they sell out.
  • Go on a weekday and arrive before ~8:30 AM; Multnomah's lot fills fast.
  • Closed in 2026: Eagle Creek Trail & Wahclella Falls remain closed from the Dec 2025 storms — check the Forest Service site before you go.
  • Some trailheads need a Northwest Forest Pass ($5/day).
TUE · JUN 16 Mount Hood & Trillium Lake Day Trip · Easy Loop
The Plan

About 90 minutes southeast (fuel up in Sandy — last reliable gas). First, the flat, accessible Trillium Lake Loop (~2 mi, boardwalks, no climbs) for the classic mirror-image of Mt. Hood on the water. Then up to Timberline Lodge at 6,000 ft — a 1937 National Historic Landmark (and The Shining's exterior), free to walk in, free summer parking. Optional easy Magic Mile chairlift for alpine views without the hike.

WED · JUN 17 Museums, Curiosities & Riverfront Rest-ish · City
Choose your speed

A flexible city day. For art & history: the Portland Art Museum and the tranquil Lan Su Chinese Garden. Hands-on: OMSI (science museum with a real submarine on the river). Wonderfully weird: the Freakybuttrue Peculiarium and Movie Madness (a video store with a Hollywood-prop museum inside).

Evening

Dinner at Kati Portland egg-free easy (modern Thai) or Top Burmese egg-free easy. Music fans: check the historic Crystal Ballroom (famous bouncing floor) or Revolution Hall.

THU · JUN 18 The Oregon Coast — Cannon Beach & Haystack Rock Day Trip · Beach Walk
The Plan

About 90 minutes west through forested Highway 26. Cannon Beach is the star — towering Haystack Rock, tide pools at low tide, a flat walkable beach, and galleries in town.

Nearby — pick one or two
  • Ecola State Park — dramatic headland viewpoints; a short easy stroll gets the big vista.
  • Hug Point — a hidden waterfall and sea caves at low tide.
  • Astoria (optional) — historic riverfront town with the Astoria Column view and sea lions on the docks.
Coffee

Coast pit-stops: Sleepy Monk Coffee in Cannon Beach or Sea Level Bakery.

FRI JUN 19 – TUE JUN 23 Flex Days & Favorites Before SF Open · Your Call
A menu, not a schedule

This generous stretch stays loose — rest, repeat a favorite, or pick from:

  • Mt. Tabor Park — wander an extinct volcano inside the city (gentle paths, skyline views).
  • Hawthorne & Division — bookshops, vintage, and dense good vegetarian eating.
  • A relaxed Willamette Valley / Dundee Hills afternoon (~45 min) — vineyards, farm stands, valley views.
  • Sellwood antiques row or a second Gorge / Coast outing if you loved the first.
  • The Ole Bolle troll at Nordic Northwest — one of Thomas Dambo's giant recycled-wood trolls, the only one in Oregon.
  • Saturday Jun 20: the Saturday Market again if you missed anything.
Tue Jun 23: Keep it light — pack and prep for the flight to San Francisco.
Leg Three · The City by the Bay

San Francisco

Wed Jun 24 – Sat Jun 27 · 3 nights · Fly PDX→SFO · Public transit + Uber (no car needed). Book the hotel now — World Cup demand.
WED · JUN 24 Arrive · Ferry Building & the Waterfront Half Day
Getting in

From SFO, BART runs straight downtown (~30 min, cheap) — easiest with luggage. Check into your Union Square / Nob Hill hotel (see Where to Stay).

Afternoon

Ease in along the Embarcadero to the Ferry Building Marketplace — an artisan food hall that's a vegetarian playground. Ride a historic streetcar or cable car for the classic SF moment.

Evening

Dinner at Udupi Palace egg-free easy in the Mission — pure-vegetarian South Indian, with crispy dosas the size of a briefcase. Naturally egg-free and a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

THU · JUN 25 Golden Gate Park, the Bridge & Lands End Full Day · Easy Walks
Morning — Golden Gate Park

The park's gems: the de Young Museum (art, plus a free observation tower), the serene Japanese Tea Garden, and the California Academy of Sciences (rainforest dome, aquarium, living roof).

Afternoon

Out to the coast: a Golden Gate Bridge viewpoint, then the flat, scenic Lands End clifftop trail down to the haunting Sutro Baths ruins.

Evening

World Cup note: Bay Area match tonight (~7 PM, Levi's Stadium). Downtown will be lively and Ubers may surge — reserve dinner early. Try Besharam egg-free easy, an inventive vegetarian Gujarati spot in the Dogpatch, or Greens at Fort Mason — the landmark vegetarian restaurant with bay-and-bridge views (book ahead).

FRI · JUN 26 Murals, Mission Food & the Classic Postcards Full Day · City
Morning — the Mission

The city's most colorful district: the murals of Balmy Alley and Clarion Alley, sunny Dolores Park, and a serious vegetarian food scene. Lunch at Cha-Ya egg-free easy (vegetarian Japanese) or pastries at Tartine.

Afternoon

The classic sweep: Painted Ladies at Alamo Square, the crooked Lombard Street, and Chinatown / North Beach on foot. Optional Coit Tower for the view.

Want Alcatraz? It books out weeks ahead in summer. If it appeals, reserve the moment your dates are firm — the itinerary stands beautifully without it.

Where to Stay

San Francisco — 3 nights, no car

Stay in Union Square or Lower Nob Hill — the most transit-connected part of the city (cable cars, Muni, and BART all at hand), walkable and ideal for first-timers. You won't need a car here.

Union SquareCentral hub, every transit line, biggest hotel selection across price points. Weekday rates sometimes dip — but book early given World Cup demand.
Lower Nob HillTop transit score in the city, a notch quieter, cable cars at the door.
Embarcadero / Financial DistrictNear the Ferry Building & BART; business-district hotels can drop prices on weekends.

Seattle — 3 nights, parking required

Base yourself downtown / Belltown or near Seattle Center — walkable to Pike Place and the Space Needle. Prioritize a property with on-site or bundled parking; street parking is scarce and garages run $45–65/night.

Belltown / DowntownClosest to Pike Place & the waterfront; widest hotel choice. Confirm the parking rate when booking — it's often separate.
Lower Queen Anne / Seattle CenterSteps from the Space Needle & Chihuly; slightly calmer, often easier parking.
South Lake UnionNewer hotels, frequently with garages; a short ride to the core.
Book Seattle early: the July 1 Round-of-32 match at Lumen Field will tighten availability and lift prices for the nights of June 30 and July 1 especially. Locking this in is now one of your two most urgent tasks (alongside SF).

Packing for a PNW summer

Layers. Late-June/early-July mornings can be cool, afternoons warm; the coast and Mt. Hood run colder and breezier than the city. Pack a light rain shell, comfortable walking shoes, and sunglasses. Multnomah Falls will mist you — a thin waterproof layer helps.

  • Flights: Mom & Sister into SEA (Jun 11) & out of SEA (Jul 2); the family's PDX⇄SFO round-trip (Jun 24 / Jun 27).
  • Columbia River Gorge timed-entry permit (~$2) for your chosen Gorge day.
  • Dinner reservations — Greens (SF), Café Flora / Harvest Beat (Seattle), and any World Cup-night dining.
  • Alcatraz — only if you want it; books out weeks ahead.
  • Check trail status — Forest Service site for Gorge closures (Eagle Creek / Wahclella still closed in 2026).
