⚽ Read this first: the 2026 World Cup overlaps your entire trip

The FIFA World Cup runs across North America this summer, and both Seattle and the San Francisco Bay Area are host cities — squarely inside your travel window. This won't ruin anything (it can even be a fun bonus), but it will affect hotel prices, crowds, and traffic, so plan around it:

  • Seattle (Jun 11–13): You arrive before Seattle's matches begin (the city's first match is June 15 at Lumen Field). Good timing — but the city is already buzzing, so book your hotel now.
  • San Francisco (Jun 24–27): There's a Bay Area match the evening of Thursday June 25 at Levi's Stadium (Santa Clara, ~40 mi south). Expect region-wide hotel demand, fan zones downtown, and surge pricing on Ubers. Book your SF hotel immediately — this is the single most time-sensitive thing on the list.
  • Silver lining: Free fan festivals and big-screen watch parties pop up in both cities — a lively, low-cost evening if you want a taste of the atmosphere.

Your Dietary Key

Every food pick below is vegetarian. The one nuance I've planned around: Mom & Sister avoid eggs in savory food (desserts with egg are fine), while you eat eggs freely.

Spots marked egg-free easy have plenty of fully egg-free savory mains — think South Indian, Sri Lankan, Ethiopian, Thai, Chinese, and Gujarati kitchens, where egg-free is the default rather than a special request. At any other listed spot, egg-free mains exist but are worth a quick confirm with the server. I've leaned the dinner picks toward the easy ones so nobody's ever stuck reading fine print.
Leg One · The Emerald City

Seattle

Thu Jun 11 – Sat Jun 13 · 2 nights · You drive up from Portland to collect Mom & Sister, then explore before heading south.
THU · JUN 11 Arrivals & an Easy First Evening Light
Morning

Leave Portland early — it's about a 3-hour drive up I-5 to Sea-Tac. Aim to arrive with a little buffer before Mom & Sister land.

Afternoon

Pick up at the airport, then check into your hotel and let everyone shake off the travel. After a long flight, today is intentionally gentle — no agenda beyond a gorgeous, gentle reintroduction to the Northwest.

Evening

A short stroll around Pike Place Market as it winds down, then an easy dinner nearby — Plum Bistro egg-free easy in Capitol Hill is a beloved all-vegan kitchen, so there's zero menu-decoding on night one.

FRI · JUN 12 Markets, Glass & Skyline Views Full Day
Morning

Back to Pike Place Market early, when it's liveliest and least crowded — the flying fish, the original Starbucks, and the flower stalls. Grab pastries at the Piroshky Piroshky window (the apple-cinnamon rolls and veggie hand-pies are the move).

Midday — Seattle Center

Walk or hop the monorail to Chihuly Garden and Glass — luminous blown-glass art indoors and in the garden, and a clear favorite for an art-loving group. Pair it with the Space Needle observation deck, or skip the ticket and just enjoy the grounds. Art-and-music fans can add MoPOP (Museum of Pop Culture) next door.

Late Afternoon

Taxi up to Kerry Park for the postcard skyline-and-Mt.-Rainier view, then over to the Fremont Troll (the giant under-the-bridge sculpture) and a wander through quirky Fremont.

Evening

Dinner at Café Flora egg-free easy — a 30-year Seattle institution in a light-filled garden room, vegetarian with abundant egg-free and vegan mains. (Save room; their weekend cinnamon rolls are legendary if you return for brunch.)

SAT · JUN 13 Waterfront, History & the Drive South Half Day + Drive
Morning

A relaxed final loop: the Olympic Sculpture Park (free, open-air, easy flat paths along the water) and a stroll through historic Pioneer Square for the red-brick architecture. Coffee and a bite before you roll out.

Afternoon

Point the car south for the ~3-hour drive to Portland. Leaving today gets you out ahead of Seattle's first World Cup match (June 15) and onto home turf. Settle in, order in, rest up.

Seattle parking tip: Most downtown/Belltown hotels charge $45–65/night for valet or garage parking. Factor that in, and consider a hotel that bundles parking. See Where to Stay below for picks.
Leg Two · The City of Roses & Its Wild Backyard

Portland Base

Sat Jun 13 – Wed Jun 24 · Home base · A mix of city days, gentle day trips by car, and built-in rest. June is peak rose season and prime waterfall flow.
SUN · JUN 14 Roses, Gardens & the World's Best Bookstore City · Gentle
Morning — Washington Park

You've timed this perfectly: mid-June is peak bloom at the International Rose Test Garden (free, gentle terraced paths, sweeping city views). Right above it, the Portland Japanese Garden is one of the finest outside Japan — serene, beautifully maintained, and an art-and-nature highlight.

Afternoon

Drive up to Pittock Mansion for the panoramic view over the city to Mt. Hood, then downtown to Powell's City of Books — a full city block of books, color-coded by room. Easy to lose a happy hour here.

Evening

Dinner at Mirisata egg-free easy, a worker-owned Sri Lankan curry house (rice & curry plates, jackfruit, naturally egg-free), or Lalibela egg-free easy for communal Ethiopian platters scooped with injera.

MON · JUN 15 Columbia River Gorge — Waterfall Alley Day Trip · Easy Walks
The Plan

Thirty minutes east and you're in one of America's great waterfall corridors. Drive the Historic Columbia River Highway, stopping at Vista House at Crown Point (sweeping gorge view), then a string of falls with short, mostly flat paths: Latourell Falls, Bridal Veil Falls (easy quick walk), and the icon — Multnomah Falls, with the photogenic Benson Bridge a short stroll up. Add neighboring Wahkeena Falls if you have energy.

Lunch

Continue to Hood River for lunch and a walk through the riverfront town, then loop home.

⚠️ 2026 Gorge logistics — don't skip

  • Timed-entry permit (~$2) is required to drive the Waterfall Corridor late May–early Sept. Book online in advance — they sell out.
  • Go on a weekday and arrive before ~8:30 AM; Multnomah's lot fills fast.
  • Closed in 2026: Eagle Creek Trail and Wahclella Falls remain closed from the Dec 2025 storms — skip those and check the Forest Service site before you go.
  • Some trailheads need a Northwest Forest Pass ($5/day).
TUE · JUN 16 Slow Portland — Neighborhoods, Carts & Coffee Rest-ish · City
The Idea

A deliberately unhurried day after the Gorge. Wander the Alberta Arts District and Mississippi Avenue — indie shops, murals, and Portland's famous food-cart pods (endless vegetarian options in one spot, so everyone picks their own).

Coffee & Quirk

Dip into Portland's coffee religion. For pure whimsy, the late-night Rimsky-Korsakoffee House — dessert and espresso in a creaky Victorian where the tables quietly move. Or visit the McMenamins Kennedy School, an old elementary school turned brewpub-hotel-cinema; you can walk the art-filled halls and grab a drink in the former detention room.

WED · JUN 17 Mount Hood & Trillium Lake Day Trip · Easy Loop
The Plan

About 90 minutes southeast (fuel up in Sandy — last reliable gas). First stop, the flat, accessible Trillium Lake Loop (~2 mi, boardwalks, no climbs) for the classic mirror-image of Mt. Hood on the water — easy enough for everyone and pure postcard.

Midday

Up to Timberline Lodge at 6,000 ft — a 1937 National Historic Landmark (and The Shining's exterior). Free to walk in and explore the lobby and views; summer parking is free. Optional easy ride up the Magic Mile chairlift for alpine views without the hike.

Optional scenic return: loop back via Hood River and the Gorge if everyone's got the energy — otherwise the direct route home is shorter.
THU · JUN 18 The Oregon Coast — Cannon Beach & Haystack Rock Day Trip · Beach Walk
The Plan

About 90 minutes west through forested Highway 26 to the coast. Cannon Beach is the star — the towering Haystack Rock, tide pools at low tide, and a flat, walkable beach. Browse the galleries and shops in town.

Nearby — pick one or two
  • Ecola State Park — dramatic headland viewpoints; a short easy stroll gets you the big vista (the longer Clatsop Loop is optional).
  • Hug Point — walk to a hidden waterfall and sea caves at low tide.
  • Astoria (optional extension) — historic riverfront town with the Astoria Column view and sea lions on the docks.
Coffee

Coast pit-stops: Sleepy Monk Coffee in Cannon Beach or Sea Level Bakery on the way.

FRI · JUN 19 Museums, Curiosities & Riverfront Rest-ish · City
Choose your speed

A flexible city day. For art & history: the Portland Art Museum and the tranquil Lan Su Chinese Garden downtown. For hands-on fun: OMSI (science museum with a real submarine docked on the river). For the wonderfully weird: the Freakybuttrue Peculiarium (a tongue-in-cheek oddities museum) and Movie Madness (a video store with a Hollywood-prop museum inside).

Evening

Dinner at Kati Portland egg-free easy (modern Thai, easy egg-free mains) or Top Burmese egg-free easy. Music fans: check listings at the historic Crystal Ballroom (famous bouncing floor) or Revolution Hall.

SAT · JUN 20 Saturday Market + Wine Country (Optional) Flex · Gentle
Morning

The Portland Saturday Market on the waterfront (and the year-round PSU Farmers Market) — crafts, local food, easy browsing.

Afternoon — optional

A scenic, low-key swing through the Willamette Valley / Dundee Hills (~45 min): rolling vineyards, farm stands, and relaxed tasting rooms with valley views. Skip it freely if a slow day at home sounds better — that's the whole point of the flex day.

SUN JUN 21 – TUE JUN 23 Flex Days & Favorites Before SF Open · Your Call
A menu, not a schedule

These days are intentionally open — repeat a favorite, rest, or pick from:

  • Mt. Tabor Park — wander an extinct volcano inside the city, with reservoirs and skyline views (gentle paths).
  • Hawthorne & Division — bookshops, vintage, and one of the city's densest stretches of good vegetarian eating.
  • Sellwood antiques row, or a stroll along the Eastbank Esplanade river path.
  • A second Gorge or Coast outing if you loved the first.
  • The Ole Bolle troll at Nordic Northwest — one of artist Thomas Dambo's giant recycled-wood trolls, the only one in Oregon.
Tue Jun 23: Keep it light — pack and prep for the early-ish flight to San Francisco.
Leg Three · The City by the Bay

San Francisco

Wed Jun 24 – Sat Jun 27 · 3 nights · Fly PDX→SFO · Public transit + Uber (no car needed). Book the hotel now — World Cup demand.
WED · JUN 24 Arrive · Ferry Building & the Waterfront Half Day
Getting in

From SFO, BART runs straight downtown (~30 min, cheap) — easiest with luggage. Check into your Union Square / Nob Hill hotel (see Where to Stay).

Afternoon

Ease in along the Embarcadero to the Ferry Building Marketplace — an artisan food hall that's a vegetarian playground (cheese, bread, produce, chocolate). Ride a historic streetcar or cable car for the classic SF moment.

Evening

Dinner at Udupi Palace egg-free easy in the Mission — pure-vegetarian South Indian, with crispy dosas the size of a briefcase and a great thali. Naturally egg-free and a guaranteed crowd-pleaser for Mom & Sister.

THU · JUN 25 Golden Gate Park, the Bridge & Lands End Full Day · Easy Walks
Morning — Golden Gate Park

Spend it among the park's gems: the de Young Museum (art, plus a free observation tower), the serene Japanese Tea Garden, and — for a science-loving group — the California Academy of Sciences (rainforest dome, aquarium, living roof).

Afternoon

Out to the coast: a Golden Gate Bridge viewpoint, then the Lands End trail (flat, scenic clifftop walk) down to the haunting Sutro Baths ruins — one of the city's best low-key, low-cost outings.

Evening

World Cup note: there's a Bay Area match tonight (~7 PM, Levi's Stadium in Santa Clara). Downtown will be lively and Ubers may surge — reserve dinner early. Try Besharam egg-free easy, an inventive vegetarian Gujarati spot in the Dogpatch, or Greens at Fort Mason — the landmark vegetarian restaurant with picture-window bay-and-bridge views (book ahead).

FRI · JUN 26 Murals, Mission Food & the Classic Postcards Full Day · City
Morning — the Mission

The city's most colorful district: the murals of Balmy Alley and Clarion Alley, sunny Dolores Park, and a serious vegetarian food scene. Lunch at Cha-Ya egg-free easy (vegetarian Japanese — soba, udon, sushi) or grab pastries at Tartine.

Afternoon

The classic sweep: Painted Ladies at Alamo Square, the crooked Lombard Street, and Chinatown / North Beach on foot. Optional Coit Tower for the view.

Want Alcatraz? It books out weeks ahead, especially in summer. If it appeals, reserve the moment your dates are firm — otherwise the itinerary stands beautifully without it.
SAT · JUN 27 A Last Look & Fly Home Half Day
Morning

One easy final stop near your hotel — a cable-car ride you missed, or coffee and people-watching at the Ferry Building — then BART back to SFO for the flight to Portland.

Leg Four · Home Stretch

Portland, Unhurried

Sat Jun 27 – Fri Jul 3 · Home base · Slow days, favorites revisited, and the run-up to the July 4th weekend.
JUN 27 – JUL 3 Six Open Days — A Curated Menu Flex · Gentle
The philosophy

By now everyone knows the city, so this stretch stays loose on purpose. Mix rest days with a few of these:

  • Anything you loved, again — a return Gorge waterfall run, a second beach day at the coast, or a lazy afternoon at a food-cart pod.
  • Gardens & views — the parts of Washington Park or the Japanese Garden you didn't finish; sunset from Pittock Mansion or Mt. Tabor.
  • Deeper coast — an overnight or long day to Manzanita or Oswald West State Park (Short Sand Beach), quieter than Cannon Beach.
  • Wine & farm country — a relaxed Willamette Valley afternoon if you skipped it earlier.
  • July 4th lead-up — the city's energy builds toward the holiday; riverfront strolls and neighborhood celebrations are easy to dip into. (Main fireworks fall on the 4th, just after your window — so no need to plan around crowds.)
Fri Jul 3: A gentle final day — a favorite breakfast, last coffee, a slow goodbye to the Northwest before Mom & Sister head out.

Where to Stay

Seattle — 2 nights, parking required

Base yourself downtown / Belltown or near Seattle Center — walkable to Pike Place and the Space Needle, and central for the short visit. Prioritize a property with on-site or bundled parking, since street parking is scarce and garages run $45–65/night.

Belltown / DowntownClosest to Pike Place & the waterfront; widest hotel choice. Confirm parking rate when booking — it's often separate.
Lower Queen Anne / Seattle CenterSteps from the Space Needle & Chihuly; slightly calmer, often easier parking.
South Lake UnionNewer hotels, frequently with garages; a short ride to the core.

San Francisco — 3 nights, no car

Stay in Union Square or Lower Nob Hill — the most transit-connected part of the city (cable cars, Muni, and BART all at hand), walkable and well-suited to first-timers. You won't need or want a car here.

Union SquareCentral hub, every transit line, biggest hotel selection across price points. Weekday rates sometimes dip — but book early given World Cup demand.
Lower Nob HillTop transit score in the city, a notch quieter, cable cars at the door.
Embarcadero / Financial DistrictNear the Ferry Building & BART; business-district hotels can drop prices on weekends.
Why book SF first: the June 25 Bay Area match plus general tournament tourism will tighten availability and lift prices region-wide. Locking the hotel is the most urgent task on your list.

Packing for a PNW summer

Layers. June mornings can be cool and gray, afternoons warm; the coast and Mt. Hood run colder and breezier than the city. Pack a light rain shell, comfortable walking shoes, and sunglasses. Multnomah Falls will mist you — a thin waterproof layer helps.

Booking checklist — in priority order

  • San Francisco hotel (Jun 24–27) — most urgent; World Cup demand. Union Square / Lower Nob Hill.
  • Seattle hotel with parking (Jun 11–13) — Belltown / Seattle Center.
  • PDX⇄SFO flights — Jun 24 out, Jun 27 back.
  • Columbia River Gorge timed-entry permit (~$2) for your chosen Gorge day.
  • Dinner reservations — Greens (SF), Harvest Beat (Seattle) and any World Cup-night dining.
  • Alcatraz — only if you want it; books out weeks ahead.
  • Check trail status — Forest Service site for Gorge closures (Eagle Creek / Wahclella still closed in 2026).
